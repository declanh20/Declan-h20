Who I Am
THE STORY
SO FAR

My name is Declan and I'm a 12-year-old competitive swimmer from Queensland, Australia. I've been in the water since I was 2 years old, and competitive swimming has been my passion since I was 9.

I train at Trinity Aquatic under dedicated coach Anthony Heath, putting in over 10 hours per week in the pool alongside dryland conditioning. Every session, every lap, and every early morning is another step toward my goals.

My specialty events are freestyle, backstroke, and butterfly, racing distances from 50m through to 200m. I'm the Queensland Long Course Champion in the 50m Freestyle, with podium finishes across backstroke events, and I'm currently working toward qualifying and competing at Australian National Age Championships.

This page is here to document the journey — the hard work, the sacrifices, the achievements, and everything in between — while connecting with coaches, sponsors, brands, and organisations that believe in supporting the next generation of Australian swimming talent.

PATH TO SWIMMING
AUSTRALIA

Here's what it actually takes for a Queensland junior swimmer to reach the national stage — and exactly where Declan sits on that journey right now.

Stage 01 Complete
Club Competitive Racing
Join a Swim Queensland affiliated club and compete in weekly club carnivals and interclub meets. Build race craft, technique, and competitive mentality. The foundation everything else is built on.
3+ sessions/week Club membership Swim QLD registration
✓
Stage 02 Complete
QLD Regional Championships
Qualify by hitting Swim Queensland time standards for your age group and region. Compete across 7 QLD regions in age group events. The top 16 finishers in each event advance to State Age Championships. Requires 8–12 hrs/week training commitment.
QLD time standard ✓ 8–12 hrs/week Top 16 to advance 7 Regions QLD
✓
Stage 03 Complete
QLD State Age Championships
The pinnacle of Queensland age group swimming. Held annually, this is where the state's best junior swimmers compete. Top performers earn selection to represent Queensland at the Australian Age Championships. Demands 10–15+ hrs/week.
State qualifying standard 10–15+ hrs/week Represent QLD

The Team Behind the Times
COACHED TO
COMPETE

Behind every fast time is a great coach. Declan trains under dedicated coaching staff at Trinity Aquatic who bring elite-level knowledge to every session — from race strategy and stroke mechanics to mental preparation and taper planning.

These poolside moments between athlete and coach are where the real work happens. Breaking down a race, adjusting technique, building the mental toolkit. The relationship between Declan and his coaching team is one of the most important ingredients in his rapid development.

At 12 years old, Declan is still in the early stages of his athletic career — and the foundation being built now through quality coaching will define what's possible at 16, 18, and beyond. 🇦🇺
